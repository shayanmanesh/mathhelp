Introduction and Real-World Applications

Calculus is the mathematical study of continuous change. It's the foundation for understanding motion, growth, optimization, and countless phenomena in science, engineering, and economics.

Why Learn Calculus?

  • Physics & Engineering: Model motion, forces, and energy
  • Economics & Finance: Optimize profits, analyze market trends
  • Medicine & Biology: Model population growth, drug dosages
  • Computer Science: Machine learning, graphics, optimization
  • Data Science: Statistical modeling and analysis

The Two Branches of Calculus

Differential Calculus: Studies rates of change (derivatives)

Integral Calculus: Studies accumulation of quantities (integrals)

$$\text{Fundamental Theorem: } \int_a^b f'(x)\,dx = f(b) - f(a)$$

Core Concepts and Definitions

1. Functions and Continuity

Function: A relation that assigns each input exactly one output

Continuous Function: A function with no breaks, jumps, or holes

Continuous: $f(x) = x^2 + 3x - 1$

Discontinuous: $f(x) = \frac{1}{x}$ at $x = 0$

2. Rate of Change

Average Rate of Change: Change over an interval

$$\text{Average Rate} = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$$

Instantaneous Rate of Change: Rate at a specific point (derivative)

$$f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a + h) - f(a)}{h}$$

Understanding Limits

What is a Limit?

A limit describes the value a function approaches as the input approaches a certain value.

$$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$$

Read as: "The limit of f(x) as x approaches a equals L"

Evaluating Limits: Step-by-Step

Example: Find $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}$

Step 1:

Direct substitution gives $\frac{0}{0}$ (indeterminate)

Step 2:

Factor the numerator: $x^2 - 4 = (x + 2)(x - 2)$

Step 3:

Simplify: $\frac{(x + 2)(x - 2)}{x - 2} = x + 2$ for $x \neq 2$

Step 4:

Now evaluate: $\lim_{x \to 2} (x + 2) = 4$

Important Limit Rules

  • Sum Rule: $\lim(f + g) = \lim f + \lim g$
  • Product Rule: $\lim(f \cdot g) = \lim f \cdot \lim g$
  • Quotient Rule: $\lim\frac{f}{g} = \frac{\lim f}{\lim g}$ (if $\lim g \neq 0$)
  • Special Limit: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$

Derivatives: Step-by-Step Methods

Definition of a Derivative

The derivative of a function f(x) at a point measures the instantaneous rate of change:

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}$$

Basic Derivative Rules

Power Rule

If $f(x) = x^n$, then $f'(x) = nx^{n-1}$

$f(x) = x^3$ â†’ $f'(x) = 3x^2$

$f(x) = x^{1/2}$ â†’ $f'(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}$

Product Rule

If $f(x) = u(x) \cdot v(x)$, then:

$$f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)$$

Example: Find the derivative of $f(x) = x^2 \sin x$

Step 1:

Identify $u = x^2$ and $v = \sin x$

Step 2:

Find derivatives: $u' = 2x$ and $v' = \cos x$

Step 3:

Apply product rule: $f'(x) = 2x \cdot \sin x + x^2 \cdot \cos x$

Chain Rule

If $f(x) = g(h(x))$, then:

$$f'(x) = g'(h(x)) \cdot h'(x)$$

Or in Leibniz notation: $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$

Common Derivatives

Function Derivative
$\sin x$ $\cos x$
$\cos x$ $-\sin x$
$e^x$ $e^x$
$\ln x$ $\frac{1}{x}$
$a^x$ $a^x \ln a$

Integrals: Techniques and Applications

Definition of an Integral

Indefinite Integral: The antiderivative of a function

$$\int f(x)\,dx = F(x) + C$$

where $F'(x) = f(x)$ and C is the constant of integration

Definite Integral: The area under a curve

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

Basic Integration Rules

Power Rule for Integration

$$\int x^n\,dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

Special Case

$$\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx = \ln|x| + C$$

Integration by Substitution

Example: Evaluate $\int 2x(x^2 + 1)^3\,dx$

Step 1:

Let $u = x^2 + 1$

Step 2:

Find $du = 2x\,dx$

Step 3:

Substitute: $\int u^3\,du$

Step 4:

Integrate: $\frac{u^4}{4} + C = \frac{(x^2 + 1)^4}{4} + C$

Integration by Parts

$$\int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du$$

Example: Evaluate $\int x e^x\,dx$

Step 1:

Let $u = x$ and $dv = e^x\,dx$

Step 2:

Then $du = dx$ and $v = e^x$

Step 3:

Apply formula: $xe^x - \int e^x\,dx$

Step 4:

Final answer: $xe^x - e^x + C = e^x(x - 1) + C$

Practice Problems (Progressive Difficulty)

Beginner Level - Limits

  1. Evaluate: $\lim_{x \to 3} (2x + 1)$
  2. Find: $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x^2 + 3x}{x}$
  3. Calculate: $\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x}$
  4. Determine: $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}$

Intermediate Level - Derivatives

  1. Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x) = 3x^4 - 2x^2 + 5x - 1$
  2. Differentiate: $y = x^2 \cos x$
  3. Find $\frac{dy}{dx}$ if $y = \ln(x^2 + 1)$
  4. Calculate: $\frac{d}{dx}[e^{3x} \sin x]$

Advanced Level - Integrals

  1. Evaluate: $\int x \sqrt{x^2 + 1}\,dx$
  2. Find: $\int \frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx$
  3. Calculate: $\int_0^{\pi} x \sin x\,dx$
  4. Solve: $\int \frac{x^3}{x^2 + 1}\,dx$

Applications

  1. Find the area between $y = x^2$ and $y = 2x$ from $x = 0$ to $x = 2$
  2. A particle moves with velocity $v(t) = 3t^2 - 6t$. Find the displacement from $t = 0$ to $t = 4$
  3. Find the volume of the solid formed by rotating $y = \sqrt{x}$ about the x-axis from $x = 0$ to $x = 4$
  4. Optimize: Find the dimensions of a rectangular box with volume 1000 cubic units that minimizes surface area
