üìú The Rich History of Mathematics

3000 BCE

Ancient Babylonian Mathematics

The Babylonians developed the first sophisticated number system and created the foundation for algebra. Their base-60 system still influences how we measure time and angles today.

Algebra Origins
300 BCE

Euclid's Elements

Euclid's "Elements" became the foundation of geometry for over 2,000 years. This masterwork introduced logical proofs and the axiomatic method that still shapes mathematics today.

Geometry
1637

Birth of Modern Calculus

Isaac Newton and Gottfried Leibniz independently developed calculus, revolutionizing mathematics and enabling the scientific revolution. Their work laid the groundwork for modern physics and engineering.

Calculus
1654

Probability Theory

Pascal and Fermat's correspondence about gambling problems led to the birth of probability theory, which now underpins statistics, economics, and data science.

Statistics
